## What languages do you cover?

**English to Japanese only**. Focusing on a single language pair helps me maintain consistency and quality.

## How do I get a quote?

Send your document (or word count and subject matter) to [contact@soma-patent-translator-jp.com](mailto:contact@soma-patent-translator-jp.com). Include your target deadline with timezone. **Reply within 24 hours** with a detailed quote. **Pricing and terms are flexible** based on project specifics.

## What payment methods do you accept?

Wise, PayPal, and bank transfer.

## What is your turnaround time?

- Under 5,000 words: **1.5 business days**
- Up to 12,000 words: **3 business days**
- Larger documents: custom timeline
- **Rush delivery available** on request

## What types of documents do you translate?

**Patent specifications** (descriptions, claims, abstracts, drawings text) and **standalone patent abstracts**, for both Paris route and PCT national-phase Japan filings.

**Primary fields**: medical devices and mechanical engineering.

**Secondary fields**: electronics, semiconductors, telecommunications, software, AI, optical, and lighting technologies.

## Is my document kept confidential?

Yes. Processed through **AWS Bedrock in Japan only** (Tokyo region, ap-northeast-1), with **no data retention** and **no use in AI training**. **NDA signed before we start**. [Full security details →](/how-i-work/#confidentiality--data-security)

## How does your AI-assisted workflow affect quality?

AI accelerates the first draft, but **quality comes from human review**. I check every sentence for accuracy, nuance, and patent-specific conventions. The system also enforces terminology consistency across your filings, so **quality compounds project to project**. [More on my workflow →](/how-i-work/)

## How do you handle non-native English source documents?

7+ years of patent work have built both **technical fluency** and **a reviewer's eye for non-native English** — often translated from German, Chinese, or French before reaching me. I **reconstruct the technical intent** behind structural irregularities and render it in **clean Japanese**.

## Do you work with translation agencies?

Yes, when **the agency allows me to use my own translation system**.

I'm not a fit for agencies requiring specific CAT tool mandates.

## Where are you based?

Kyoto, Japan (JST / UTC+9). Clients worldwide.
